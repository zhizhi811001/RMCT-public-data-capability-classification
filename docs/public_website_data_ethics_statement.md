\# Public Website Data Ethics Statement



This project uses publicly accessible manufacturer website information to study regional manufacturing capability visibility and partner discovery.



\## Scope of Data Use



The study focuses on manufacturing capability information intentionally disclosed by firms on public websites. This includes information such as:



\- manufacturing processes;

\- materials handled;

\- product or component types;

\- certifications and quality systems;

\- sectors served;

\- engineering and product-development support;

\- digital and automation capabilities;

\- delivery and commercial readiness signals;

\- sustainability and circular-production practices.



The study does not aim to profile individuals, evaluate personal behaviour, or collect private information.



\## Public Data and Research Purpose



The website data were collected for academic research into manufacturing capability visibility. The purpose of the analysis is to understand how publicly available company information can support supplier discovery, regional capability mapping and supply-chain visibility.



Only publicly accessible pages were considered. No login-protected, private, paid-access or password-protected website areas were used.



\## Privacy Protection



Although company websites may contain personal names, email addresses and phone numbers, these are not the focus of the study. Where detected during preprocessing, personal names, email addresses, phone numbers and direct contact details were removed or excluded from shared research outputs.



The repository does not intentionally release:



\- personal names;

\- individual job titles;

\- email addresses;

\- phone numbers;

\- direct contact details;

\- contact-form content.



\## Copyright and Website Text



Manufacturer websites may be protected by copyright and website terms of use. For this reason, the repository does not redistribute full raw website HTML or full cleaned website text.



Instead, the repository shares only derived and limited research outputs, including:



\- short evidence spans;

\- website URLs where available;

\- crawl dates;

\- document hashes;

\- RMCT labels;

\- anonymised company-level capability profiles;

\- aggregate benchmark results.



This supports reproducibility while reducing the risk of inappropriate redistribution of website content.



\## Evidence Spans



Short evidence spans are provided only where needed to support verification of assigned capability labels. These spans are intended to show the basis for classification decisions, not to reproduce website pages.



Evidence spans are limited in length and should be interpreted as research evidence for capability classification.



\## Company-Level Outputs



The company-level capability profiles in this repository describe publicly observable manufacturing capability signals. They should not be interpreted as complete assessments of a firm's full operational capacity, commercial performance, financial status, or supplier suitability.



The profiles indicate what was publicly visible from available website evidence at the time of collection.



\## Limitations



Public website data are incomplete and uneven. Some firms disclose extensive capability information, while others provide limited or outdated information. Absence of evidence in the dataset does not mean absence of capability in practice.



The classification outputs should therefore be treated as public-data-derived indicators rather than definitive supplier evaluations.



\## Responsible Use



Users of this repository should:



\- avoid using the data for unsolicited marketing or direct commercial targeting;

\- avoid treating RMCT classifications as definitive supplier qualifications;

\- verify supplier information directly before procurement decisions;

\- respect website owners' rights and terms of use;

\- cite the repository and associated manuscript if using the data or code.



\## Contact



For questions about data ethics or responsible use of this repository, please contact the corresponding author of the associated manuscript.
