# Public Website Data Ethics Statement

This study uses publicly accessible company website information to assess public capability observability, not confidential operational capability, live capacity, commercial availability, or verified supplier performance. Website evidence was treated as public self-disclosure by organisations and was interpreted as evidence that a firm publicly associates itself with a capability. A website match does not establish that the capability is currently available, certified, priced, staffed, or suitable for a specific procurement decision.

The study minimised redistribution of third-party website content. Full raw and cleaned website texts are not shared. Public release is limited to derived data, anonymised company profiles, short evidence spans where needed for validation, controlled vocabulary terms, document hashes, benchmark outputs and methodological metadata. Personal names, email addresses, telephone numbers and similar contact details were removed during preprocessing where detected and are not intentionally included in the released dataset.

The analysis is conducted at the organisation level. It does not profile private individuals, infer sensitive personal characteristics, or use personal contact details for decision making. Public website evidence is used only to classify disclosed manufacturing, engineering, quality, sector, digital, commercial and sustainability-related capability signals.

The dataset should not be used as a supplier approval, certification, compliance or operational-capacity register. Users should treat RMCT profiles as pre-qualification evidence and verify current availability, certification status, quality compliance, capacity, price and delivery conditions directly with firms or through authoritative registers before making procurement or investment decisions.

The crawler/extraction window recovered from available metadata is 2026-06-03 18:23 to 2026-06-04 10:05. Website content may have changed since collection. The public repository therefore includes document hashes and source metadata where available to support provenance checking without redistributing full third-party text.
